RESPIRATION IMPEDIMENT BY FERMENTATION Owing to the abundance of glucose available in the medium, Saccharomyces cerevisiae shows a pronounced impairment of
respiratory capability. This was discovered by Crabtree and become known as the Crabtree effect which is interpreted as the repression of one energy source (respiration) by another fermentation biomass while most of the cell constituents such as lipids, polysaccharides and proteins are synthesized from sugar.
